# What to Include in a Freelance Contract: The Complete Checklist

A freelance contract without the right clauses is worse than no contract — it gives you false confidence while leaving you exposed. Here's exactly what every freelance contract needs.

## The Non-Negotiable Sections

### 1. Scope of Work

This is the most important clause. Define exactly what you will and won't deliver. Vague scope leads to scope creep — clients keep adding "small" requests that add up to weeks of unpaid work.

Be specific: "5 pages of website copy, including Home, About, Services, Blog, and Contact" — not just "website copy."

### 2. Payment Terms

Specify the total project fee, deposit amount (typically 25–50% upfront), payment schedule, due dates, and late payment fee. Never start work without a deposit.

### 3. Revision Policy

Define how many rounds of revisions are included and what constitutes a revision versus new work. Without this clause, a client can request unlimited revisions indefinitely.

### 4. Cancellation and Kill Fee

What happens if the client cancels mid-project? A standard kill fee: work completed to date is billed at your hourly rate, plus a 20% kill fee on remaining work.

### 5. IP Transfer Clause

Intellectual property rights should transfer to the client only upon receipt of full payment. This gives you leverage if a client refuses to pay.

### 6. Governing Law

Specify which state's laws govern the contract. This prevents confusion if you and your client are in different jurisdictions.

## Profession-Specific Clauses

### For Web Developers

- Hosting handover terms and credential delivery
- 30-day bug fix warranty (not new features)
- Client responsibility for ongoing third-party subscription costs

### For Photographers

- Gallery delivery timeline
- Print release terms — what client can and cannot do with images
- Cancellation policy: 48+ hours notice = no penalty, same-day = up to 100% fee

### For Consultants

- Confidentiality clause protecting client's business information
- Non-solicitation terms
- Specific deliverables definition

### For Designers

- Number of revision rounds included
- File formats to be delivered
- License vs. full ownership of designs
