# Payment Reminder Email Templates for Freelancers

Late payments are the biggest cash flow problem for freelancers. A good reminder email at the right time gets you paid faster without damaging the client relationship. Here are templates for every stage.

## Template 1: 3 Days Before Due (Friendly Reminder)

**Subject:** Friendly reminder — Invoice #[number] due [date]

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Just a quick heads-up that Invoice #[number] for [amount] is due on [date].
>
> No action needed if you've already arranged payment — just wanted to give you advance notice.
>
> [View Invoice →]
>
> Thanks, [Your Name]

**Tone:** Warm, non-demanding. Assume good faith.

## Template 2: Due Today

**Subject:** Invoice #[number] is due today

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> This is a reminder that Invoice #[number] for [amount] is due today.
>
> Please let me know if you have any questions about processing this payment.
>
> [View Invoice →]
>
> Thanks, [Your Name]

**Tone:** Direct but professional.

## Template 3: 7 Days Overdue

**Subject:** Following up on Invoice #[number] — 7 days overdue

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Invoice #[number] for [amount] is now 7 days past due. I haven't heard back from my previous message.
>
> Please arrange payment today or let me know if there's a problem so we can resolve it.
>
> If payment is not received soon, I may need to apply a late fee per the terms of our agreement.
>
> [View Invoice →]
>
> [Your Name]

**Tone:** More direct. Mention the late fee clause if it exists.

## Template 4: Final Notice

**Subject:** Outstanding invoice — action required

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Invoice #[number] for [amount] is significantly overdue. Despite previous reminders, I have not received payment or a response.
>
> Please arrange payment immediately or contact me to discuss. If I do not receive a response within 3 business days, I will need to pursue further action.
>
> [View Invoice →]
>
> [Your Name]

**Tone:** Formal. Make clear you are serious without burning the bridge.
