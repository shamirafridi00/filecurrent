# HoneyBook Alternatives in 2026 — 5 Cheaper Options for Freelancers

HoneyBook raised its Starter plan price from $19 to $36 per month in early 2025 — an 89% increase with almost no notice. If you're one of the thousands of freelancers reconsidering your tools, this guide covers the best alternatives.

## Why Freelancers Are Leaving HoneyBook

The price increase caught most users off-guard. HoneyBook positioned it as part of a feature expansion, but for the majority of freelancers who only used contracts, invoices, and e-signatures, the new $36/month price tag made no sense.

Freelance communities on Reddit saw a wave of "what should I switch to?" posts. The consensus: most freelancers don't need 80% of what HoneyBook offers. They need four things — contracts, invoices, e-signatures, and payment reminders.

## What to Look for in an Alternative

Before comparing tools, get clear on what you actually need:

1. **Contract creation** — profession-specific templates save hours
2. **Invoice generation** — clean, professional, with your branding
3. **E-signatures** — legally binding, ESIGN Act compliant
4. **Payment reminders** — automated, no daily cap

Everything else (project management, scheduling, CRM pipelines) is nice-to-have, not must-have.

## The 5 Best HoneyBook Alternatives

### 1. FileCurrent — $9/month

FileCurrent is the lean, affordable alternative built specifically for freelancers who want exactly these four features and nothing else. Profession-specific templates for web developers, photographers, consultants, designers, copywriters, and marketers. ESIGN Act compliant e-signatures with full audit trail. No daily reminder cap.

**Best for:** Freelancers who want HoneyBook's document features at 75% less cost.

**Free tier:** Yes — 3 documents/month, no credit card required.

### 2. Bonsai — $21/month

Bonsai offers more features than FileCurrent — including time tracking and project management — and integrates them more tightly. The trade-off is price and complexity.

**Best for:** Freelancers who also want time tracking and client portals.

### 3. Dubsado — $28/month

Dubsado is powerful but notorious for its learning curve. It's best suited for studios or agencies with complex client workflows.

**Best for:** Studios with complex, multi-step client processes.

### 4. Wave — Free

Wave is primarily an accounting tool with invoicing built in. There are no contract or e-signature features.

**Best for:** Freelancers who only need invoicing.

### 5. Google Docs + DocuSign — $0 + $15/month

The cobbled-together approach. It works, but it breaks down fast — you're managing multiple tools and manually tracking everything.

**Best for:** Absolute beginners with very low document volume.

## The Bottom Line

If you just need contracts, invoices, e-signatures, and reminders — FileCurrent at $9/month does everything HoneyBook does for the document workflow at 75% less cost. The free plan lets you try it with 3 documents per month, no credit card required.
