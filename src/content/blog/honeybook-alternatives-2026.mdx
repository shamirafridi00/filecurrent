# HoneyBook Alternatives in 2026: 5 Leaner Options for Freelancers

HoneyBook has raised its plan prices sharply over the past two years, with little notice. If you're one of the many freelancers reconsidering your tools, this guide covers the best alternatives.

## Why Freelancers Are Leaving HoneyBook

The price increases caught most users off guard. HoneyBook positioned them as part of a feature expansion, but for the majority of freelancers who only used contracts, invoices, and e-signatures, the higher price made little sense.

Freelance communities on Reddit saw a wave of "what should I switch to?" posts. The consensus: most freelancers don't need 80% of what HoneyBook offers. They need four things, namely contracts, invoices, e-signatures, and payment reminders.

## What to Look for in an Alternative

Before comparing tools, get clear on what you actually need:

1. **Contract creation:** profession-specific templates save hours
2. **Invoice generation:** clean, professional, with your branding
3. **E-signatures:** legally binding, ESIGN Act compliant
4. **Payment reminders:** automated, no daily cap

Everything else (project management, scheduling, CRM pipelines) is nice to have, not a must have.

## The 5 Best HoneyBook Alternatives

### 1. FileCurrent: $15/month

FileCurrent is the lean, affordable alternative built specifically for freelancers who want exactly these four features and nothing else. Profession-specific templates for web developers, photographers, consultants, designers, copywriters, and marketers. ESIGN Act compliant e-signatures with a full audit trail. No daily reminder cap.

**Best for:** Freelancers who want HoneyBook's document workflow for far less.

**Free trial:** Yes. A 5-day free trial of every feature, no credit card required.

### 2. Bonsai: $21/month

Bonsai offers more features than FileCurrent, including deeper project management, and integrates them more tightly. The trade-off is price and complexity.

**Best for:** Freelancers who also want time tracking and client portals.

### 3. Dubsado: $28/month

Dubsado is powerful but notorious for its learning curve. It's best suited for studios or agencies with complex client workflows.

**Best for:** Studios with complex, multi-step client processes.

### 4. Wave: Free

Wave is primarily an accounting tool with invoicing built in. There are no contract or e-signature features.

**Best for:** Freelancers who only need invoicing.

### 5. Google Docs + DocuSign: $0 + $15/month

The cobbled-together approach. It works, but it breaks down fast, because you're managing multiple tools and manually tracking everything.

**Best for:** Absolute beginners with very low document volume.

If you just need contracts, invoices, e-signatures, and reminders, FileCurrent at $15/month does everything HoneyBook does for the document workflow at a fraction of the cost. Every account starts with a 5-day free trial of every feature, no credit card required.
